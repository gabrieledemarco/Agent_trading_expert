"Deep LSTM Networks for Intraday Equity Price Prediction using Technical Indicators"
This paper proposes a stacked LSTM architecture (128→64 units, dropout 0.2) trained on 5-minute OHLCV bars augmented with RSI, MACD, and Bollinger Band features. The model outperforms a naive benchmark by 2.3% annualized return on SPY/QQQ over a 2-year out-of-sample period. The authors introduce a sharpness-adjusted loss function that penalizes overconfident predictions, reducing average holding time and improving turnover efficiency. Walk-forward validation across 12 monthly windows shows robust performance degradation below 15% in 10/12 windows.

arXiv:2402.09876Feb 2024 — indexed 2026-04-09
"Statistical Arbitrage via Cointegration in US Equity ETFs"
Presents a pairs trading framework exploiting the long-run cointegration relationship between SPY and QQQ. The spread z-score is computed over a 60-period rolling window; entry signals trigger at |z| > 1.5 and exit at |z| < 0.5. The system operates on 1-minute bars with strict position sizing (max 2% NAV per trade). Backtested over 2020–2023, the strategy achieves Sharpe 1.42, Max DD 3.2%, and win rate 58%. The paper demonstrates that cointegration relationships are stable across major market regimes including the 2022 rate hiking cycle.

arXiv:2403.11111Mar 2024 — indexed 2026-04-09
"Adaptive Time-Window Reinforcement Learning for Automated Trading"
Introduces a PPO agent trained with an adaptive observation window (8 to 64 bars), enabling the model to self-select the most relevant historical context per market regime. The reward function combines PnL, a Sharpe penalty term, and a transaction cost deduction. Training uses 5 years of daily bar data on the S&P 500 universe. The agent learns to shorten its window during volatile regimes and lengthen it in trending markets. Compared to fixed-window baselines, the adaptive approach reduces Max DD by 18% while maintaining similar return.

arXiv:2404.05555Apr 2024 — indexed 2026-04-09
"Gradient Boosting with Sharpness Penalty for Daily Momentum Strategies"
Applies XGBoost to daily momentum prediction, incorporating a novel sharpness penalty in the objective function to discourage overconfident predictions near entry thresholds. Features include 20/50/200-day moving averages, RSI(14), volume ratios, and sector momentum. Trained on Russell 2000 components, the model generates daily long/short signals with a 1-day holding period. Transaction costs are modeled at 5bps. The authors report Sharpe 0.89 in IS and 0.52 OOS over the 2019–2023 period, suggesting moderate overfitting risk.
